Starting university is a big step in life, it can be kind of scary. When taking this next step its important to have all the imformation you can.
thats whay at the University og Newcastle we have gathered a list of questions current students wish they had known teh answers to before they began their joyrney with us

How do I know what classes to choose?

If you go to the course you are doing on the main UoN site and find your course handbook you will find a guide that will assist you in planning out what classes you you are required to do when to do them

Where do I go on my first day?

Simply find your time table and attend your first class in what room and what time you are meant to be there. if you need assistance in locating your room please see the Map on this site

How do I choose my classes?

When you enroll in the university you will be directed to a sight called "MyHub" which allows you to choose your classes.

How do I find my classroom?

Just see the map on our Map page on this site to help you find your classrooms.

Who is my course co-ordinator?

it all depends on what course you are doing, but if you would like to find out please visit the Staff page.

How do I contact my lecturer?

when you are enrolled in a certain class, the blackboard will give you all the contact information you will need to get in touch with your lecturer.

What is blackboard?

Blackboard is where you will be doing all of your online learning. It is where you will receive course material and all assesments, It is where you will get notifications about classes and is where you will receive your marks. Blackboard is very important so familiarise yourself with it.

What do I need on my first day?

for your first day the only things you need are yourself and a device like a laptop or just a plain old notebook thats how you prefer to take notes in your lecture, a textbook if its required which you can find out on blackboard.

What time do I start classes?

Unlike highschool, university doesnt have a set start and end time, your time table will tell you what classes you have on what day and once they have finnished you can leave for teh day.

Is university like school and I have to be there all day every day?

at university you only need to show up at the time your classes are schedualed for on your timetable

What is HECS-HELP?

This is a program set up by the Australian government that allows students to goto university and not have to worry about paying anything back until they have a job and have finnished your degree.

How much is my limit for HECS-HELP?

For 2020, the HELP loan limit is $106,319. If you plan to study medicine, dentistry or veterinary science leading to initial registration, or eligible aviation courses the HELP loan limit is $152,700.

When do I pay it back?

You're not required to pay back anything until you are earning above the threshold, then it its automatically deducted from your pay.

Do I need to pay for parking?

Luckily at the Central coast campus no you don't as parking is free. However, if you goto the Newcastle campus you will need to pay for parking, order a parking pass.

Do I need special circumstances to qualify for HECS-HELP?

To qualify for the program the following rules must apply to you:
- Be studying in a Commonwealth supported place.
- Be an Australian citizen and meet the residency requirements (you must study at least part of your course in Australia).
- Be a New Zealand Special Category Visa (SCV) holder or permanent humanitarian visa holder and meet the residency requirements.
- Submit the Request for Commonwealth support and HECS-HELP form to your provider by the census date.
- Be enrolled in each unit/subject at your provider by the census date.
- Not have already borrowed up to your HELP loan limit.
